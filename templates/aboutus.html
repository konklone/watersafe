The goal of H2Osafe is to inform citizens of water contaminations in their area and action on it by contacting their representatives.

What am I seeing on the search results ?

In the US, 85% of population is served water through a public water system (PWS). The rest 15% use private sources of water like wells etc. Congress passed the Safe Drinking Water Act (SDWA) in 1974 to protect the quality of American drinking water by regulating the nationâ€™s public drinking water supply. Each PWS is checked for over 90 different contaminants like nitrate, arsenic etc and any violation in the contaminant level is reported to the public. h2oSafe has compiled this information for each county and have ranked them in their state.

What is a PWS (Public Water System) ? What does it have to do with water in my tap ?

A PWS is one that serves piped water to at least 25 persons or 15 service connections for at least 60 days each year. The source of water for a PWS is either

  1. Groundwater
  2. Surface water like rivers, lakes and reservoirs
Although most of the PWS use groundwater as their source (127,000 PWS uses groundwater in comparison to 13,000 that uses surface water) , most of population is served through surface water 209.4 million compared to 97.5 million served through groundwater.
